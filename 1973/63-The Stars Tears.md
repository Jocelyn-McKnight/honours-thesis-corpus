Planti taim long moningtaim yumi save lukim liklik wara i stap long lip bilong diwai na long ston na long gras.

Long dispela liklik wara ol tumbuna i save tok olsem, Dispela liklik wara i stap long lip bilong diwai na long ston na long gras, em i gat stori bilong en.

Sem tasol i mekim dispela wara i kamap.'

Olsem na ol tumbuna i stori olsem: Bipo tru, mipela i no kamap yet, long dispela taim sta i hangamap long skai na em i toktok wantaim wesan.

Tupela i resis long wanpela tok, olsem na tupela i laik traim.

Long dispela taim Wesan em i bikhet.

Em i tokim Sta, i spik, Yupela i kamap bikpela lain pinis.

Yumitupela yet i mas kaunim namba bilong yumitupela.

Namba bilong husat i kamap bikpela tru?

Namba bilong husat i win?

Sta i harim dispela tok, na em i bekim tok, i spik, Gutpela, yumitupela i mas kaunim namba bilong yumitupela.'

Orait, nau Sta na Wesan tupela i kirap mekim dispela wok long kaunim namba bilong tupela.

Wesan i bikhet tru na em i tok, `Sta, mi laik kaunim namba bilong lain bilong yu pastaim.

Bihain yu ken kaunim namba bilong lain bilong mi.'

Orait, Sta i harim dispela tok na em i wet i stap.

Wesan i tok long kaunim namba bilong sta na i kaunim olgeta sta i hangamap i stap long skai.

Nau Sta i tok, i spik, Gutpela, yu kaunim namba bilong mi pinis.

Nau mi laik stat wok na kaunim namba bilong lain bilong yu.'

Sta i tok olsem, na Wesan i slip.

Wesan i bikhet na em i slip tru.

Sta i kaunim kaunim namba bilong Wesan.

Tasol Wesan em i planti tumas, olsem na sta i no inap kaunim em olgeta.

Sta em inap kaunim Wesan long ples klia.

Tasol insait long graun em i no inap tru.

Olsem na wesan i winim sta, na Sta em i sem.

Em i sem tru, na wara i kamap long ai bilong em na i kapsait.

Olsem na long moningtaim yumi save lukim wara bilong ai bilong sta i stap long gras na long ston na long lip bilong diwai.

Long ago there was a man who lived in a village near the sea.  

He had two sons.  

The father was very old, and his two sons were strong young men.  

One night they went outside and saw many stars in the sky.  

The younger son said, “Father, those stars are beautiful. I want to go and live among them.”  

The father laughed and said, “You cannot go there, they are very far away.”  

But the younger son did not listen.  

He climbed a very tall tree so that he could reach the sky.  

He climbed higher and higher until he disappeared.  

His brother waited for him but he never came back.  

The next morning the brother saw water on the grass and leaves.  

He knew that these were the tears of his brother who had gone up to live among the stars.  

And now, when we see dew in the morning, we say it is the tears of the stars.
