
Em hia liklik sotpela stori bilong ol tumbuna na i go olsem.

Long wanpela bus tru ol planti ol yangpela man ol i stap na wanpela maunten tu i stap.

Na long as bilong dispela maunten i gat wanpela bus haus bilong wanpela man i save kaikai man.

Orait, ol yangpela man ol i stap antap long maunten na ol i rausim ol bikpela ston na i na i go daun na brukim haus bilong dispela bikpela man i save kaikai ol man.

Orait, ol i mekim olsem i go planti taim.

Na wanpela taim bel bilong em i hat tru na lukautim ol i go antap na painim ol i stap na hait tru na lukim ol i go na lukim pes brata bilong ol.

Na em i kilim em na karim i go daun long haus bilong em na kukim kaikai na bungim bun bilong em long haus bilong em na i stap.

Na ol brata bilong dispela man ol i no pret long dispela pasin i bin kamap long brata bilong ol.

Nau ol tu i go na rausim ston gen.

Orait, ol i mekim olsem i go na bikpela man i pinisim ol yangpela man.

Na wanpela las brata bilong ol tasol i stap.

Nau wanpela taim em i krai i go na i go daun long haus bilong bikpela man na em i hait gut tru na lukim dispela man i kam daun na i go long gaden bilong em.

Nau dispela brata bilong ol yangpela man em i go insait na lukim bun bilong ol brata bilong em i bungim i stap long wanpela hap na em i kisim na pulimapim ol long wanpela bilum.

Orait, em i kukim haus bilong dispela bikpela man na em i go pinis long wanpela hap tru.

Na em i lukim wanpela gutpela raunwara na em i bungim ol dispela bun bilong ol brata bilong em na putim long raunwara.

Na em i go i stap wanpela mun na em i go lukim dispela bun.

Ol i sting na i kamap sampela rokrok na i stap i go.

Wanpela mun moa i pinis na em i go lukim na ol rokrok i kamap olsem pes bilong man na em i belgut na givim ol kaikai i go i stap.

Bihain long wanpela mun gen ol i kamap liklik man na wanpela taim em i laik kisim ol, tasol, ol i tok, Mipela i no bikpela 
yet.'

Na em i go winim mun na em i go lukim ol i gat lek.

Nau em i kisim ol i go long haus bilong em na ol i stap gut na i kamap planti yangpela man gen.

Na ol i stap i go na skin bilong ol i strong.

Nau ol laik kilim dispela man na ol i lukautim em i go na kilim indai dispela man na ol i stap gut tru.

This is a short story about the ancestors.  

In the deep forest there were many young men who lived on top of a mountain.  

At the base of this mountain there was a house that belonged to a cannibal.  

The young men who lived on top of the mountain threw some boulders down the mountain and destroyed the house that belonged to the huge cannibal.  

They did this not once, but often.  

One time, the cannibal became completely enraged, so he began to search on top of the mountain for these young men.  

He saw the first brother and killed him, then carried him down to his house.  

He cooked and ate him, then he collected the bones that remained and hung them up inside his house.  

The remaining brothers were not afraid of what had happened to their brother.  

So, again they threw some boulders down the mountain.  

After this, the huge cannibal came back and finished off all of the young men but one.  

So then the last brother cried and cried and then he went down to the huge man’s house.  

He hid himself well and then saw the man go into his garden.  

So then the remaining brother went inside the house and saw the bones of his brothers together in one part of the house.  

He gathered them all up and put them into his net bag.  

He then set fire to the giant’s house and set off for another place.  

He came upon a nice pond where he took the bones of his brothers and put them into the pond.  

He left the pond for a month and then returned to look for the bones.  

The bones had decayed and become frogs in the pond.  

Another month passed and he returned to the pond to see that the frogs now had the faces of men.  

He was happy to see this and fed the frogs with some food.  

Later after another month had passed, the frogs had become small men.  

The brother wanted to take them with him, but they said to him, “No, we are not fully grown yet.”  

Yet another month passed, and the brother returned to the lake to look for them.  

Now he was able to take them with him back home where they lived happily and became young men again.  

They lived there and when their bodies became strong, they wanted to kill the huge man.  

They looked for him, then killed him and lived happily after that.  

**Author:** Tinam Sorewe
